Interesting Facts About Movies

Motion pictures were first made in the 1890s as a result of the production of moving picture cameras. The first moving picture "The Roundhay Garden Scene" was made in 1888 by Louis Le Prince. Short films were at first usually silent, but to make up for this a band would sometimes play live music. The panoroma shot was developed in 1987 and marked a big advancement in filmmaking. Early cameras filmed at 16 frames per second (FPS), with modern cameras now filming at 25 FPS. The first feature-length film "The Story of the Kelly Gang" was made in 1906. It was over an hour long with a reel length of 4000 feet. The first movie theatres opened in 1907. The Titanic movie was released in 1997 when movies could only be projected by film reels. The 3 hour 15 minute movie was 17.7 reel longs and over 17,700 feet of film. Movie theatres now often use digital video projectors, using the technology DLP or Digital Light Processing. Movie studios can now send videos via the internet, satellite or hard drive.

Box Office Success

Avatar, released in 2009 remains the highest grossing movie of all time, with a worldwide gross of $2.9 billion. Avengers: Endgame (2019) is the second highest grossing movie, with a worldwide gross of $2.8 billion. The sequel to Avatar, Avatar: The Way of Water (2022) became the third highest grossing movie with a worldwide gross of $2.3 billion. Titanic (1997) is the fourth highest grossing movie with a worldwide gross of $2.3 billion and Star Wars: Episode VII - The Force Awakens is the fifth highest grossing movie with a worldwide gross of $2 billion.
